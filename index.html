The 3 Email Angles

Plain text. Under 100 words. One CTA.

A
Capacity Hook
Subject: Quick question about [Facility Name]
Short, curiosity-driven. Opens with a genuine question about their current situation. Low-pressure, high-relevance. Works because operators who are at capacity are actively thinking about this.
Test First
B
Revenue Math
Subject: $80–150/unit/month without construction
Leads with the outcome. Operators understand ROI language. Makes the business case before asking for time. Most effective with operators who have empty land sitting idle.
High Intent
C
Proof of Work
Subject: Sketched something out for [Facility Name]
Includes a personalized 60-second Loom video showing their facility layout in the container configurator. Even weak copy gets 3–5× more replies when a video is attached. Highest effort, highest reward.
Highest Reply Rate
📄
Plain text only
No logos, no HTML, no images. The email should look like it came from a person. Logos signal mass marketing — reply rates drop immediately.
🎯
One CTA per email
Not "visit our website or call us or reply." Just: "Worth a 10-minute call?" Confusion kills conversion.
⚡
Under 100 words
Operators are busy. Long emails get deleted. Say what you need to say in the first three sentences and stop.
🤐
Never anchor on price
When they ask "what's the cost?" — don't answer with a number. Answer with a question: "Depends on your setup — how many units are you thinking about?"
